Reconnecting Detroit through freeway removal

As the state of Michigan considers spending $1.8 billion to add one lane to less than seven miles of I-94 in inner-city Detroit, the rest of us can find alternatives to such extensive investment that repairs and strengthens the city not just to mitigate, but to undo, the mistakes of the past. De-Spur Detroit is series of simple study proposals to remove two freeway spurs that bracket downtown Detroit, the Lodge and I-375, in the most straightforward manner allowed.

1 / The Lodge

A new boulevard flows traffic from the Lodge into Corktown and the southern edge of downtown before transitioning to Jefferson Avenue through a new gateway park. In this scenario, Cobo Center is reconstructed to fit within the street grid without sacrificing floor area. The proposal also preserves the few remaining historic structures that border the Lodge.
